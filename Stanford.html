Stanford University

Description: Stanford University is a private university located in Stanford, California. It is known for its impressive academics as well as its selectivity. It is ranked as one of the world's top universities.

Quick Facts

  • Location:Stanford, California
  • Status: Private
  • Size(numbers from 2019): 16,520 students (7,083 undergraduates and 9,437 graduates)/2,240 faculty(17 Nobel laureates)
    • 5:1 student to faculty ratio
  • 7 Schools
    • Business
    • Earth, Energy, & Enviromental Sceinces
    • Education
    • Engineering
      • Note: This is the school where a major in Architecture is found.
    • Humanities & Sciences
    • Law
    • Medicine
  • 8,180 contiguous acres
  • ~700 major buildings

Fun Facts/Pros

  • Stanford has a long tradition of "fountain hopping" (jumping in water fountains around campus).
  • Stanford's mascot is a tree ("The Stanford Tree") and the costume is designed new every year.
  • Stanford has a Cactus Garden in which some of the original plants (planted between 1880-1883) have been restored and are surviving to this day.
  • Motto: "Die Luft der Freiheit weht" - Ulrich von Hutten ("The wind of freedom blows.")
  • Frederick Law Olmstead (known for Central Park) oversaw the planning and architecture of the campus.
    • Speaking of the campus and its architecture, it is absolutely beautiful.
  • Stanford's school colors are Black, White, and "Cardinal" (so basically the same colors as Gracie at NCSU).

...Weird Facts

  • Full Moon is celebrated on campus. Although the event is less formal now (usually including music and performances) female freshman would traditionally line up as male seniors would kiss them and give them a rose.
